Section 4

Authentication — SPF · DKIM · DMARC

The three DNS-based protocols that verify sender identity and message integrity.

SPF
Sender Policy Framework · RFC 7208
Validates that the IP used to send an email is authorised for the sender's domain. The domain publishes permitted IPs in a DNS TXT record.
✓ Pass: sending IP authorised · ✗ Fail: DMARC policy applies
DKIM
DomainKeys Identified Mail · RFC 6376
Adds a cryptographic signature proving the message originated from an authorised server and was not modified in transit.
✓ Valid: message integrity confirmed · ✗ Invalid: DMARC applies
DMARC
Domain-based Message Authentication · RFC 7489
Ties SPF and DKIM together and instructs receivers on what to do on failure. Enables aggregate and forensic reporting.
p=reject is strongest — eliminates spoofed domain email entirely
